Experiences Courtney Ng



Girls Who Code Summer Immersion Program (2018)


During the summer, I was accepted into a coding program that empowers girls and creates a sisterhood among the girls. Within the program we were offered many opportunities to meet many different people as well as gain connections with the people we meet. It has built a sense of community through all the people who were involved in the summer program. Career wise, it gave a little more definition and helped solidify what I was interested in and my capabilities through it all.

Urban Arts: School of Interactive Arts (2018 - Present)


In my experience so far I have learned an incredible amount about ways of thinking about different problems and how to approach them in a certain mindset. In addition to that, it has given me the chance to work on many projects that relate to hobbies that I enjoy a lot. Junior Reserve Officers Training Corps (2016 - Present)


Staff 6 Technology and Communications


In my staff, currently I hold the positiion of Non-Commissioned Officer in Command Senior (NCOIC). Some of the various tasks that we do are set up for a multitude of events. Some of the events we go to the sound booth in our school and we set up videos to preview, projectors, mixers, microphones, walkie talkies, audio, and lighting. Other events are similar set ups but more portable by not using the auditorium speakers and setting up different types of amplifiers. Other duties include maintaining the website for our Patriot Battlion and fixing and maintaining the quality of the equipment that JROTC owns such as desktops, laptops, printers, copiers, televisions, and any other appliance listed above.
